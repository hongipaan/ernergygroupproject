Scenario 1 : Roof PV Panel

Roof PV panel is a common installation mode for building solar panels. It produces high electricity, but it affects the appearance of the building.

The left image shows the best way to install rooftop solar panels in industrial buildings. In this scenario, taking a 1000 square meter installation as an example, within a 25 year cycle, PV panels will produce 2614MWh. After deducting installation and maintenance costs, a profit of SGD 8000 can be generated

This installation mode is suitable for situations where the roof slope of a building is relatively gentle (less than 10 degrees), and BIPV cannot be installed on the facade.

Scenario 2：BIPV

BIPV (Building Integrated Photovoltaics) is the integration of solar panels into building structures to generate electricity. Compared with Roof PV panel, BIPV systems can be more expensive upfront and require specialized installation expertise, but it hardly affects the appearance of the building

The left image shows the best way to install BIPV in industrial buildings. In this scenario, taking a 1000 square meter installation as an example, within a 25 year cycle, BIPV will produce 27,849 MWh. After deducting installation and maintenance costs, a profit of SGD 85,230 can be generated

This intallation mode is suitable for buildings which has a high quality of appearance.

Scenario 3 :  Roof PV Panel + BIPV

The left image shows the best way to install both kinds of PV panel in industrial buildings. In this scenario, taking a 2000 square meter installation (1000sqm of roof PV panel and 1000sqm of BIPV)  as an example, within a 25 year cycle, PV panels will produce 30,462MWh. After deducting installation and maintenance costs, a profit of SGD 93,230 can be generated.

This installation mode is suitable for buildings which are intended to have higher electricity production
